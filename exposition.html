Sites I like

Atom.io

Atom is a text editor created by GitHub that is simple and easily configurable. Their site is clear, simple and easy to read, as well as having some really nice scaling graphics that adapt nicely to varying screen sizes.
The site's inbuilt function to detect and change the landing page based on your operating system is also a nice touch for user friendliness, rather than needing to go through all the possible options.
The web designers did a great job of keeping the "clean" feeling of GitHub while also making a unique website that stands out.

Reddit.com

Reddit is a social media platform unlike most others and with a unique reputation. The home page helps convey all the information a user needs while also not feeling too cluttered, thanks to its rather large gutters and neatly organized tiles and filters.
Reddit's use of colors to attract attention to points of interaction also helps point the user to actions they can do, encouraging users to participate in the many communities that exist under Reddit.

Sites I dislike

4chan.org

4chan is another social media (of sorts), that is known for it's controversial /b/ and /pol/ boards. It's been around for a long time, and it shows. The site's design feels washed out and bland, and the large gutters and cluttered links, unlike Reddit, feels really awkward to navigate.
In the boards themselves, it is difficult for a user to follow a thread smoothly, not to mention feeling equally bland and uninteresting. It also does not make use of many (if at all), modern web technologies, further giving it its dated feel.
In a way, this is kind of the point of 4chan, but the fact remains that it is not a well-designed site.

phdinparenting.com

Like 4chan, PHD in Parenting is another blast from the past. Although the site is certainly usable, it doesn't make interactable elements very clear and this might confuse a visitor.
It also doesn't scale very nicely and has no responsiveness for smaller screens, meaning mobile users will most likely be quickly deterred and will move on.
